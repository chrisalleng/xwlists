{% extends "layout.html" %} {% block content %} The ListJuggler search supports a subset of the Whoosh query language. The general format of a search is something like:

s=Han AND (s=awing OR s=z95)

This will search for list that brought Han Solo and an AWing or a Headhunter

You have to give hints to ListJuggler to tell it what you are looking for. It understands ships, pilots, and upgrades.

To find a ship, you can say ship=yt1300 or s=yt1300.

To find a pilot, you can say pilot=Han or p=han.

To find an upgrade, you can say upgrade=Outrider or u=outrider

A few other hints to help you along:
  • You can use either the long, English name of something, or its canonical name. The full list of searchable names can be found on the xws specification page
  • If you have a search expression with spaces in it -- for example, "Push the Limit" -- use the canonical xws name instead, "pushthelimit"
  • Wildcards are used behind the scenes - for example, if you want to search for every list with Han and Chewbacca "p=Han AND p=Chew" will work.
  • If you don't specify an AND clause, it is inferred. "p=Han p=Chew" is the same thing as the above example.
